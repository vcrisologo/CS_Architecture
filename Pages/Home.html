Architecture is derived from the Latin word "architectura" or the Greek word "Arkhitekton" in which Arkhi means "chief" and tekton means "builder". In definition, it is the art and science of designing buildings and other physical structures. A wider definition often includes the design of the total built environment from the macro level of town planning, urban design, and landscape architecture to the micro level of construction, details and, sometimes, furniture (All About Architechture, 2018).

Types of Architecture

There are many types of Architecture that existed in the past such as Gothic, Baroque, Victorian Architecture, etc. Gothic Architecture, just like the Barouque Architechture, is a type of European Architecture. This one lasted for almost 4 centuries, starting from the mid-12th century to the 16th century. This type of architecture mainly focuses on masonry buildings and is characterized by cavern-like interior designs.

Baroque Architecture on the other hand, is another type that originated in Italy around the 17th century. It got popular enough to spread althroughout Europe and even in Western areas such as the US. This type of architecture was one of the most creative types I believe. With its design being full of art like drawings or paintings (specially on the ceiling), it's no wonder that it got widespread in Europe.

We also have the Victorian Architecture which is a type of Architecture that was developed during Queen Victoria's reign. This type appealed to the Romantic idea during the victorian period that in designing things and structures, they should prioritize beauty rather than practicality.

Now while we have historical Architecture, we also have new types of Architecture in our present day. For instance, are Minimalist and Maximalist Architecture. Mainimalist Architecture, from the word minimalist, the aim of this type is to design as minimal and simple as possible. Meanwhile, Maximalist Architecture is the total opposite. In this type, the aim is to be as maximal as possible, designing structures in a way that shows or describes "abundance".

Summary

In summary, Architecture is continuously developing and evolving from time to time. The mentioned types above are only a small portion of the actual number of types of Architecture.
